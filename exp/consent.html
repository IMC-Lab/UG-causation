Welcome to Our Study!    

This consent form asks you to take part in a research study. The study is being conducted by Felipe De Brigard, PhD., associate professor at the Department of Philosophy, the Center for Cognitive Neuroscience, and the Duke Institute for Brain Sciences.

Purpose: This research study seeks to help us understand the ways in which our experiences inform our causal judgments.

What you will be asked to do: This research study consists of one session. You will be asked to play a short game where you split a sum of money, and then answer a few questions about the game you played. The total time for participating in this research study is expected to take between 20 and 50 minutes. At the end of the study, you will be paid at a prorated rate of $12/hour, with the opportunity of earning a bonus of up to $10. Participation in this study is voluntary, and you can stop at any time without penalty, but you will only be compensated for your time if you get to the end of the study and submit your results. To withdraw from the study, close your browser window.

How your confidentiality will be maintained: If you choose to participate, your name will not be linked to your responses at any time, and there is no link between this consent form and your responses. All data will be securely saved at all times. All data we keep will be de-identified, indefinitely.

Benefits and Risks: This research will not benefit you personally. The researcher will be happy to discuss the details of the study with you in detail at its completion.  We know of no risks to you for participating in this study.

If you have any questions about this research, please contact the IMC Lab at imagination-study@duke.edu if you have any questions. For questions about your rights in this study, contact the Duke IRB at campusirb@duke.edu

Clicking Accept: By clicking on the “I consent” button, you indicate that you are 18 years of age or older, that you voluntarily agree to participate in this study and that you understand the information in this consent form. You have not waived any legal rights you otherwise would have as a participant in a research study.
